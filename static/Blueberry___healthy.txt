Healthy Blueberry Crops:
1. Soil: Use acidic, well-drained soil (pH 4.5-5.5) with organic matter.
2. Water: Maintain consistent moisture with drip irrigation.
3. Fertilization: Apply fertilizers for acid-loving plants, avoiding excessive nitrogen.
4. Pest/Disease Control: Monitor pests and diseases; use organic controls.
5. Pruning: Prune annually to promote new growth and improve fruiting.
6. Mulching: Use mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
7. Spacing: Space plants for good air circulation and sunlight.
8. Variety Selection: Choose high-yielding, disease-resistant varieties.

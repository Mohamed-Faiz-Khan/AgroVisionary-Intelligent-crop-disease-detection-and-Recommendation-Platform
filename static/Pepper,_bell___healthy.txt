Healthy Bell Pepper Crops:
1. Soil: Use well-drained, fertile soil with a pH of 6.0-6.8.
2. Water: Maintain consistent moisture, especially during flowering and fruit development; avoid waterlogging.
3. Fertilization: Apply a balanced fertilizer with emphasis on phosphorus and potassium, based on soil tests.
4. Pest/Disease Control: Monitor for pests like aphids and diseases such as blossom end rot; use integrated pest management strategies.
5. Weed Management: Control weeds to reduce competition for nutrients and water.

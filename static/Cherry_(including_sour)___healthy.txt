Healthy Cherry Crops (Including Sour Cherries):
1. Soil: Prefer well-drained, loamy soil with a pH of 6.0-7.0.
2. Water: Ensure regular watering, especially during fruit development; avoid waterlogging.
3. Fertilization: Use a balanced fertilizer based on soil tests; avoid over-fertilization.
4. Pest/Disease Control: Monitor for pests like aphids and diseases such as brown rot; implement integrated pest management.
5. Pruning: Prune annually to improve air circulation, remove dead branches, and encourage fruiting.
6. Mulching: Apply mulch to retain moisture, control weeds, and maintain soil temperature.
7. Spacing: Space trees properly for good airflow and sunlight exposure.
8. Variety Selection: Choose disease-resistant varieties suitable for your climate and intended use (sweet or sour).

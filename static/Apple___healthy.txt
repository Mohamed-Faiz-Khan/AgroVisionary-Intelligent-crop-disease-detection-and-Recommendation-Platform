Healthy Apple Crops:
1. Soil Health: Use well-drained, loamy soil with a pH of 6.0-7.0.
2. Water Management: Maintain consistent moisture with drip irrigation.
3. Fertilization: Apply balanced fertilizers based on soil tests and add organic matter.
4. Pest/Disease Control: Monitor for pests and diseases; use integrated pest management.
5. Pruning: Regularly prune for air circulation and sunlight.
6. Crop Rotation: Avoid planting in the same spot annually.
7. Mulching: Use mulch to conserve moisture and suppress weeds.
8. Variety Selection: Choose disease-resistant varieties suitable for your area.
